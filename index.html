About Me

Welcome! I'm a computational neuroscientist based in Chicago. I am a prospective graduate student seeking programs to pursue Ph.D.-level research on translational medical research. I am deeply interested in neurodegenerative diseases and am passionate about leveraging computational methods to advance our treatment of these conditions. Currently, I work as a computational research assistant where I build simulation models, software, and packages that advance our understanding of vocal development in parrots and songbirds in the BIRDS (Behavior Integrated with Data Science) Lab with Dr. Grace Smith-Vidaurre at Michigan State University.

With a background in data science, graphic design, and rigorous scientific research, I bring a unique perspective to every project I work on. I believe in the power of mutual collaboration, continuous learning, and intterdisciplinary approaches to solve complex problems. I am always eager to connect with like-minded individuals and organizations and strive to create work that makes a difference.
